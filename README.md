# DMart-Data-Analysis-
DMart Data Analysis Repository explores retail insights using DMart’s dataset. It covers customer spend, basket size, premium vs mass segmentation, underperforming categories, peak sales hours, and forecasting. Includes data cleaning, INR formatting, and dashboards with Excel/Python for actionable business decisions.

# Project Objective-
This project marks my first step into the tech world, where I analyze DMart’s retail dataset to understand consumer behavior. The objective is to study shopping patterns during the first week of January 2025, focusing on customer spending, category performance, and sales trends. Through this analysis, I aim to build foundational skills in data cleaning, visualization, and insight generation, while demonstrating how data can guide business decisions such as promotions, inventory planning, and customer engagement.

# Dataset Used-
This analysis uses DMart’s sample dataset covering the first week of January 2025.

# KPI's-

1. **Total Sales** – Overall revenue generated during the first week of January 2025.  
2. **Average Transaction Value** – Mean spend per customer transaction.  
3. **Units Sold** – Total quantity of items purchased.  
4. **Revenue by Product Category** – Comparison of sales across categories (e.g., Grocery, Electronics, Clothing).  
5. **Revenue per Customer Type** – Analysis of member vs non-member spending patterns.  
6. **Peak Sales Hours or Days** – Identification of busiest shopping times.  
7. **Average Unit Price per Category** – Price trends across different product categories.  
8. **Top Customers** – Highlighting customers contributing the highest revenue.

## Process-

1. **Data Collection & Upload**
   - Imported the raw dataset from the Kaggle
   - Verified columns such as Date, CustomerID, ProductCategory, Quantity, UnitPrice, and Total.

2. **Data Cleaning**
   - Handled blank cells using Excel functions and filters.
   - Formatted values with INR currency symbols.
   - Created calculated fields (e.g., Total Sales, Average Transaction Value , Total Transaction per day using "COUNTIF").

3. **Exploratory Analysis**
   - Used PivotTables to summarize sales by category, customer type, and date.
   - Applied Conditional Formatting to identify peak sales days and hours.
   - Checked unit price variations across categories.

4. **Visualization**
   - Built charts (bar, line, scatter, Pareto) to represent:
     - Revenue by product category
     - Top customers
     - Peak sales hours
     - Premium vs mass category segmentation
     - Average Transaction Per Day
     - Download Dashboard Here: <a href="https://github.com/mishraricha25/DMart-Data-Analysis-/blob/main/DMart_Dashboard.pdf">Dmart Dashboard</a>

5. **Insights Generation**
   - Highlighted top customers driving revenue.
   - Identified underperforming categories needing promotions.
   - Analyzed consumer behavior patterns during the first week of January 2025.

6. **Conclusion**
- **Clothing & Peronal Care** are the top revenue‑generating categories, while **Electronics** underperform.  
- **Premium and Member customers** spend more per visit and have larger basket sizes compared to Regular customers.  
- **Peak shopping activity** is concentrated at specific hours/days, though midnight peak requires validation.  
- **Daily sales fluctuate**, showing minor weekly spikes that suggest opportunities for targeted campaigns.  
- **High‑priced categories** sell fewer units, reflecting premium positioning, while mass categories drive volume.  
- **Average spend per visit** is highest among Premium customers, reinforcing the value of loyalty programs.  
- **Underperforming categories** need promotions, bundling, or marketing support to boost sales.  
- **Top customers** contribute significantly to revenue, making personalized offers and retention strategies essential.
